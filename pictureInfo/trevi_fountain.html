Trevi Fountain

Rome, Italia.

  • Trevi Fountain, or Fontana di Trevi, is an iconic fountain in Rome that is arguably a late Baroque masterpiece. Though a fountain had existed at the location of the Trevi Fountain since ancient times, it wasn't until 1762 where Nicola Salvi and Giuseppe Pannini designed and completed it.


  • Intricately carved out of Travertine stone, the fountain depicts Oceanus, god of water, in the center niche, flanked by Abundance and Salubrity. Below the gods are a number of hippocampus and tritons adding symmetry to the fountain. At the top of the fountain sits the Papal Coat of Arms, suspended by angels.
